The Solar System consists of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. 
Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. 
Jupiter is the largest planet. 
The asteroid belt lies between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. 
Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.
Course Outcomes for this material:
CO1: Understand the composition of the Solar System.
CO2: Identify characteristics of individual planets.
CO3: Describe the location of major bodies like the asteroid belt.
CO4: Explain historical changes in planetary classification.
CO5: Apply astronomical knowledge to solve space problems.
